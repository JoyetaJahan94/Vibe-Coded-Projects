# What it is?

“Tribute to 200 Years of Tech” is a vibe coded project I had made to participate in Google I/O 2026.  The condition for the participation was that I could submit anything, a simulator or a game as long as I used Google’s AI models to code them. Since I had fre reign to create anything I wanted I decided to create a web simulator. Because I am learning to code in C and thought it would be fun to finally make something a little different than just usual black and white terminal based projects I made. 

The idea of the simulator is to showcase a journey of modern tech. From the very first innovators who gave us the stepping stones of modern technology to the current chapter of AI. Therefore, the project celebrates 5 most innovative minds, 4 groundbreaking years of innovation, 3 giants of tech, 2 languages that single handedly shifted tech and 1 new chapter that we are all experiencing now. As you might notice the project was designed like a countdown. 

# How to use it?

Navigating the simulator is very easy. Simply open the simulator. There comes an opening black screen that shows some hacker like writing on the screen. Once its done it takes you to the main “Countdown” pages. 

Once you get to the countdown pages you can click left of the screen (anywhere on the left) to go to the previous page and right of the screen to go to the next page.

There are 5 “pages”:  “

- 5 Minds that Defined Tech (Page 1)
- 4 Years that changed Tech (Page 2)
- 3 Giants that Revolutonized Tech (Page 3)
- 2 Languages that shifted Tech (Page 4)
- 1 Future awaiting Tech (Page 5)

If you are at page 1 and click on the left of the screen (to go to “previous page”) you will end up at page 5 (1 Future awaiting tech). If you are at page 5 and click on the right of the screen (to go to the “next page”) then you will end up at page 1 (5 minds that defined tech) again. So essentially the pages work as a loop. 

When you click on left or right to change to another page there comes a glitch that we might usually see in older TV screens or PCs. The fonts and colors are similar to older CRT monitor TVs. The screen also has a distinct grey lines that we might have seen in older TVs. All these are deliberate instructions given to Gemini while developing it to create a sense of nostalgia and pay true homage to tech history. 

However the initial intro screen is only seen once when you open the simulator. There is no way to get back to it unless you refresh the page. 

There are no specific exit button per say. It is an endless looped simulator. Therefore you must close the tab to close the simulator. 

There is a box right under the title of the page that can be scrolled down. It might not look extremely flattering in a big screen because it was made for mobile screen specifically. So try “Tribute to 200 Years of Tech” simulator on mobile for the best experience
